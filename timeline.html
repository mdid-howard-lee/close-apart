The Timeline

Two siblings grow up together through love, fear, competition, and distance. Childhood fades. Time moves. The question is whether family survives it.

CHILDHOOD

Shared Toys & Shared Dreams

They spend their early years together, building forts, inventing imaginary worlds, and sharing the same dreams. The older sibling sees the younger one as annoying at times, but always nearby — almost permanent.

Then, a car crash changes everything.

The younger sibling nearly dies. For the first time, the older sibling understands how terrifying loss can be. The idea of life without them suddenly becomes real.

TEENAGE YEARS

Competition Replaces Comfort

As they grow older, school pressure, friendships, expectations, and comparison begin to reshape their relationship.

The older sibling becomes busier and emotionally distracted. The younger sibling notices the distance growing little by little.

Conversations become shorter. Silence becomes easier. Instead of comfort, there is tension, jealousy, misunderstanding, and emotional exhaustion.

ADULTHOOD

Family or Strangers?

Eventually, adulthood arrives.

Responsibilities increase. Lives separate. Calls become rare. Messages become delayed. Sometimes they stop reaching out entirely.

They may still technically be family, but emotionally and physically, they become strangers.

This is the default ending of the sibling game.

Can The Ending Change?

Maybe distance is inevitable. Maybe love survives anyway. Maybe understanding someone is harder than simply growing up beside them.
